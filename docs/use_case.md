# Use Case: Scholarly Knowledge Graph for Sports Leg Injuries

## Overview

The proposed use case is the development of an application based on a Scholarly Knowledge Graph for the exploration of scientific literature about sports-related injuries of the lower limb, including the foot, ankle, knee and lower-limb muscles.

The application is not intended to provide medical diagnosis or clinical recommendations. Its purpose is to support the exploration of scientific knowledge in sports medicine by connecting papers, anatomical body parts, injury types, treatment or rehabilitation methods, authors, organizations, research projects and topics.

## Target Questions

The application aims to answer questions such as:

- Which papers study injuries affecting a specific body part, such as the knee, ankle or foot?
- Which injury types are most frequently associated with each body part?
- Which treatment or rehabilitation methods are described for a given injury?
- Which papers are similar to a selected article?
- Which authors and organizations are most active in a specific injury area?
- Which research projects or funding entities are related to a given injury topic?
- Which topics emerge from the selected corpus of scientific papers?

## Scope

The project focuses on sports medicine papers related to lower-limb injuries, especially:

- Foot
- Ankle
- Knee
- Lower-limb muscles, such as hamstrings, quadriceps, calf or soleus

The injury types considered include:

- Sprains
- Muscle strains or tears
- Tendinopathies
- Ligament injuries
- Meniscal injuries
- Overuse injuries
- Stress fractures
- Rehabilitation and return-to-play studies

## Initial Corpus

At this stage, the repository will contain an initial subset of 11 open-access papers related to sports injuries of the lower limb. Their bibliographic metadata will be documented in `data/metadata/papers.csv`.

For the final version of the assignment, the corpus will be expanded to 30 papers, following the scope defined in this use case.
